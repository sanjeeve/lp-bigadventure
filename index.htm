Lonely Planet’s Big Adventure is an exciting two day event showcasing some of the world’s most exciting and fascinating phenomena, facts and discoveries. Bringing to life Lonely Planet’s fun-packed series of Not for Parents children’s books, the event hosts a series of exhibits and workshops that will introduce children to a range of exciting subjects, from some of the world’s most exotic animals to extreme temperatures and arctic discoveries.

Lonely Planet's Not for Parents series of books are designed for children aged 8-12, however the event's activities and talks are designed to suit all ages from ages 6 and up.

Do I need a ticket?

No, Lonely Planet’s Big Adventure is free for all to attend. No ticket required. Entrance will be subject to availability, there is a limited capacity in the tent at any one time.

Is the event inside?

Yes, the event is held in a giant tipi within Victoria Park’s/Wandsworth Common’s ground.

What are Lonely Planet’s Big Adventure opening hours?

The event is open to the public from 11am – 4pm.

What if it rains or is really hot?

Lonely Planet’s Big Adventure is held in a giant tipi so come rain or shine it will be possible to enjoy the event. High winds could cause alterations to the events schedule and this will be communicated on the event’s website [insert URL]

Will my child get messy?

There is a wide choice of activities at the event. Some do have messy elements including crayons and water – so it might be a good idea to bring wet wipes.

The event says it is for children aged 8-12, can I bring my 7 year old?

Yes, the event’s activities and content is aimed at children aged 8-12 however the event is open to all and there are plenty of elements that younger and older children will enjoy.

Can I buy food at the event?

No, there is no food and drink on sale at the event, but there are cafes nearby and we will not prevent you from bringing your own food or drink to the event.

Will there be queuing at the event?

As the event is free to enter, and the event has limited capacity, there may be queuing to enter the tipi at busy periods. Once inside the event, there will be an element of queuing for more popular activities and talks.

Can I bring my dog?

No. Sorry but there are no dogs will be allowed inside the tipi.

Can I leave my child at the event?

No. Children must be accompanied by a responsible adult at the event at all times.

Is there a buggy park?

There will be an outside area where buggys can be stored. However, this is an unsupervised area and all buggys and other valuables are left at your own risk.

Are there activities suitable for disabled children?

Yes – Lonely Planet’s Big Adventure is open to all. The majority of our activities will be suitable for all abilities and we aim to ensure everything is as accessible as possible. However there may still be some activities which will not be suitable for your child’s needs and it will be up to the individual or parent to decide this. Please call or email us if you would like to discuss further.

Can I buy alcohol at the event?

No, there is no food and drink available at the event.

Can I bring alcohol to the event?

No, alcohol will not be permitted inside the tent area.

How long does the event last?

The event is open from 11am-4pm, however we advise that parents should allow approximately 1 hour for a visit in order to have time to see all of the available stands and activities. There is also a programme of talks each day which can be viewed here (we recommended arriving ahead of these talks as entry is on a first come first served basis).

Are Lonely Planet’s books available to buy at the event?

All of the books in Lonely Planet’s Not for Parents series will be available to buy at the event.

Do I have to pay for my child to take part in the activities?

No, Lonely Planet’s Big Adventure is completely free.

Is there a cloakroom?

No, visitors are responsible for their own belongings at all time at the event.
